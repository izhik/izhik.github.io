Litmus Personalize

Background

After acquiring the Kickdynamic personalization tool, the team quickly realized the product had serious usability issues that were difficult to fix. Most Kickdynamic customers needed help from Account Managers to accomplish their goals, which was sustainable for a small customer base but not scalable for a large volume of self-serve users. Additionally, the technology behind Kickdynamic was built in .NET, whereas Litmus used Rails as its standard framework. This meant the engineering team would either have to navigate unfamiliar code with a steep learning curve or rebuild using known tools for faster, more reliable development.

The second iteration of the Litmus Personalize homepage based on the collected data and customer feedback.

Initially, the Kickdynamic personalization tool had a fragmented setup: users first had to create a template in one area, then navigate elsewhere to build a campaign, start a new piece of content, and finally apply the template. Additionally, users had little visibility into the available content types or how they could be used effectively.

The problem

The newly acquired product was not scalable, suffering from severe usability issues and significant educational gaps. One of the main usability challenges was helping users quickly understand what the product actually did.

The team set a goal to design, within two quarters, a lightweight version of the Personalization product that would be intuitive and easy to use, with minimal setup and core capabilities for styling. The team decided to create in a period of two quarters a lightweight version of Personalization product that is intuitive and easy to use. Minimal set up, core capabilities for styling.

Goals

Since the launch in September, the goal was to have 375 accounts actively using Litmus Personalize by New Year's, and also to generate 1 million impressions — where an impression is counted each time an email recipient views a piece of content created by a Litmus customer.

Exploration of the end-to-end Litmus Personalize user flow when integrated into the core platform.

Design Decisions

  • The template-driven approach promised several benefits:
    • Getting customers into the editing flow more quickly
    • Providing a visual representation of the widget and its possibilities
    • Showcasing editing capabilities upfront
  • Templates had to be created for the initial four content types and for all future ones.
  • Each content type would have its own dedicated editor to keep the experience simple and modular, rather than relying on a "one size fits all" editor.

The first iteration of the Litmus Personalize homepage.

Exploration of editor's core interactions.

With the new product in development, both the Kickdynamic product (Litmus Personalize Pro) and Litmus Personalize Essentials needed to remain available for Pro customers, many of whom were confused about the difference between the two. Several design options were explored to address this, focusing on how to visually communicate the duality between the two products.

High-fidelity user flows were created, starting from the Index page for each content type and guiding users through authoring content to generating HTML. A consistent three-step flow in the Editor was maintained across all content types (at the time, still referred to as "content types," since naming hadn't been finalized). In September 2023, the product launched with four widgets, and within a year, the number of widgets grew to a dozen.

Results

  • The goal of 375 accounts actively using Litmus Personalize was not fully met, with 300 accounts achieved instead.
  • The goal of 1 million impressions was significantly exceeded, with over 10 million impressions generated — indicating a high volume of sent content.
  • Positive feedback followed the launch, although there was still a noticeable learning curve for new users.
  • Customers requested additional content types and more customization options.
  • Some technical solutions that were suitable at the MVP stage had to be reworked later as the product evolved.
  • Steady growth over two years led to 60 million impressions and more than 600 accounts actively using the product.

Next steps

We began by measuring key feature KPIs, including engagement and user behavior patterns. These insights helped us expand Litmus Personalize and build new features based on customer feedback and market trends. After launching each new content type, we tracked its performance to inform further growth and prioritize future development.
